Ethiopia

Injera is not only a kind of bread—it’s also an eating utensil. In Ethiopia and Eritrea, this spongy, sour flatbread is used to scoop up meat and vegetable stews. Injera also lines the tray on which the stews are served, soaking up their juices as the meal progresses. When this edible tablecloth is eaten, the meal is officially over.

Injera Recipe

Read

Ingredients

  1. ¾ cup teff flour (found in health food shops)
  2. 3½ cups water
  3. pinch of salt

Directions

Serve: 6 People

  1. Standing time 2 days
  2. Mix teff with the water. Leave to stand in a covered bowl for a couple of days until it starts to bubble.
  3. The mixture should be slightly sour and the consistency of crêpe batter. Stir in the salt.
  4. Add a ladle of the mixture to a pre-heated skillet and cook slowly until air bubbles rise to the top.
  5. Do not let it brown, and make sure that it is not too thick.
